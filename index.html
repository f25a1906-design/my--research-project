Mathematical Proof & Derivative Derivation

1. The Logistic Growth Function \(f(x)\)

In bioprocess models, simple exponential growth is unrealistic because nutrients, surface area, and moisture are finite. The logistic function models early slow growth, a middle exponential phase, and a terminal plateau (saturation).

We represent the fungal density as:

\(f(x) = \frac{K}{1 + A e^{-rx}}\)

Where:
• \(K\) is the carrying capacity (maximal biomass supported by the soy matrix).
• \(r\) is the specific fungal expansion rate coefficient.
• \(A = \frac{K - N_0}{N_0}\), with \(N_0\) representing the initial concentration of spores at hour zero.

2. Deriving the Rate of Change \(f'(x)\)

To find how fast the biomass changes at any instant, we calculate the first derivative with respect to time (\(x\)). Utilizing the Chain Rule on \(f(x) = K \cdot (1 + A e^{-rx})^{-1}\):

\[ f'(x) = -K \cdot (1 + A e^{-rx})^{-2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[1 + A e^{-rx}] \]
\[ f'(x) = \frac{-K}{(1 + A e^{-rx})^2} \cdot (-r A e^{-rx}) \]
\[ f'(x) = \frac{K \cdot r \cdot A e^{-rx}}{(1 + A e^{-rx})^2} \]

Expressing this back in terms of the original function \(f(x)\) yields a biologically elegant formula:

\[ f'(x) = r \cdot f(x) \left( 1 - \frac{f(x)}{K} \right) \]

This shows that the rate of change is proportional to both the current biomass amount and the fraction of resources remaining (\(1 - f(x)/K\)).

Key Calculus Highlight for Agro-Product Tech Students

By taking the second derivative \(f''(x)\) and setting it to zero (\(f''(x) = 0\)), we find the inflection point. At this point, the rate of change \(f'(x)\) achieves its absolute maximum value. Biologically, this is the transition from the exponential acceleration phase to the deceleration phase. For an SFP engineer, identifying this time parameter allows you to predict precisely when the heat generation is greatest, allowing you to cycle ventilation systems perfectly!

Local Malaysian Agro-Product Case Study

Rhizopus oligosporus Solid-State Fermentation (Tempeh Production)

MARDI Food Processing Incubators, Selangor, Malaysia

Tempeh is a highly popular, nutritious fermented staple food across Malaysia. Traditional production relies heavily on sensory assessments ("feel of heat", "color of mold") to decide processing parameters. However, modernized industrial pathways formulated by MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) utilize advanced automated growth kinetics to prevent batch spoiling and extend shelf life.

During the inoculation phase at typical local facility settings (ambient temperatures between 30°C to 34°C with controlled humilities of 80%), fungal growth dynamics match kinetic models perfectly:

Critical Process Target

Inflection Point (Max Rate)

Occurs when the mold coverage has reached exactly half the carrying capacity \(K\).

Metabolic Consequence

Peak Heat Flux

Fungal respiration peaks, releasing thermal energy to the soy cake, driving core temperatures up.

Industrial Intervention

Forced Aeration Activation

Cool, humid air must be pumped through trays to hold temperatures below 38°C to avert spore thermal shock.
